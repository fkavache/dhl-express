Nadyroba

Nadyroba ("Nadyroba Tsarsul Droshi") asks one essential question: "бҲҳбҠ• бҠ•бҲҳбҠ• бӢӯбҲғбӢөбҠ• бҠЈбҲҺ?", which translates from Tigrinya as "Who is hunting whom?".

Nadyroba is the act of searching for, following, and catching a target. Nadyroba is a one time act - either you catch the target or you don't. Either "mogynadirebs", or you "moynadireb".

The artwork shows three different "momnadyrebeli" and three different "mosanadyrebeli". They are all the same, shown in different states of tension, like the tension of a drawn bow.

Both humans and animals have only two legs, earrings, and a bow. Depending on which one is "momnadyrebeli" and which one is "mosanadyrebeli" they switch the bow and the earrings.

Another important question is whether it's actually necessary to "moynadiro" or if it's enough simply to acknowledge that there is something "mosanadyrebeli" there.
